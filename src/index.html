David Minnerly

I'm a 20-year-old programming enthusiast living in the state of New York. I aim to make a career out of server administration and network security, and in my spare time I create websites and computer programs.

I frequently work on open-source projects. All of which can be found on my GitHub.

I got my first taste of programming when I was 13. I started out by learning Lua on the online building game ROBLOX. Making my little blocky creations come to life was amazing to me, and it became the foundation for something more.

Since then I've branched out to learning about web design, picking up on HTML, CSS and JavaScript along the way. I've experimented with creating applications on AngularJS and Django, and both of them were great fun to work with.

Fireflies

Fireflies is a small application that displays a quote and background image at random every time the page is loaded.

It started as an AngularJS app. It fit my needs very well at the start, but a major drawback to my setup was that new images and quotes were cumbersome to implement.

I later fixed this by porting Fireflies to Django. This works considerably better, as now when I want to add a new image or quote, I can do it directly from the admin interface.

All of the backgrounds and quotes mean a lot to me, I picked ones that I personally liked, and because of that I hold Fireflies very near to my heart.

Echo Ridge

Echo Ridge is a 3D recreation of the hub level in Mega Man Star Force 3. I built it over the span of 3 months, and it has quickly become my favorite ROBLOX project to date.

I made the first version of Echo Ridge when I was 14, back in October of 2010. Almost 4 and a half years later, I took another look at it and felt it didn't do the original game justice.

Thanks to ROBLOX's modeling system I was able to achieve a much higher level of detail. This also opened up the possibility to remove all of the seam lines, giving the map a much cleaner appearance.

This new version is a much better representation of Echo Ridge, and it's an excellent example of the difference 4 years of experience can make. It is one of my most detailed projects on ROBLOX, and overall I'm very happy with how it turned out.
